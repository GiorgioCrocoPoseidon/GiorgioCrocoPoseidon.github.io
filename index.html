The Concept

War of the Oceans

For centuries, the ocean has endured. Pollution. Overfishing. Oil spills. The slow erasure of entire species. The deep has watched, and remembered every wound.

Then came the nuclear attack. A weapon aimed at the sea itself. It could not silence what the deep carries.

"You hit first. But the ocean struck back."

War of the Oceans is a 14-track concept album that follows the awakening of Poseidon and the ocean's mythological creatures — from the first tremor in the deep, through war, and finally the rebirth of a world returned to nature's dominion.

It is a story of consequence. Of memory. Of a world that forgot the ocean does not forget.

The Creator

Behind POSEIDON,
one mind. One vision.

Some stories wait decades to be told. Giorgio Croco's is one of them.

Milan, 1996. Talent scout at Warner Chappell Music Italy. Registered SIAE composer under the alias Jhopolaris. Two editions of Sanremo. He brought them Rhapsody. They said no. Years later, Rhapsody achieved worldwide success.

That closed door changed everything. Los Angeles — a whisky with Lemmy Kilmister at the Rainbow Bar & Grill. Conversations with Ronnie James Dio, Vince Neil, Tommy Lee. Moscow — Space Moscow, 9,000 sqm, Tiësto, Steve Aoki. Back in Italy — professional drone videographer, founder of Lido Wave.

“The music was always inside me. I just needed the right instrument to let it out.”

When AI platforms arrived with true creative customisation, something clicked. The music Giorgio had kept silent for thirty years began to surface. POSEIDON was born.

POSEIDON is not artificial intelligence making music.
It is a musician who finally found a way to make his music.
